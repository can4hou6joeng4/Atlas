# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

This project expects respectful, constructive participation. Be direct about
technical problems, but focus criticism on the work and the evidence.

Examples of expected behavior:

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experience levels.
- Give and accept constructive feedback.
- Keep discussions focused on improving the project.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, threats, or personal attacks.
- Public or private abuse of another participant.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of issues, pull requests, or discussions.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — the repository, issues,
pull requests, discussions, and any other official channels — and also applies
when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They
may remove comments, edit or reject contributions, close issues, or block
participants when behavior harms the project or its contributors. Enforcement
decisions aim to be fair, proportionate, and consistent.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the
maintainer at **can4hou6joeng4@163.com**. Reports will be reviewed and handled
as confidentially as is practical. Where possible, please include:

- What happened, with relevant context.
- Where it happened (links to issues, pull requests, or discussions).
- Any additional information that may help the review.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is a project-specific document informed by widely adopted
community norms such as the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org).
